<p><p>The Anti-reflective (AR) and Anti-fingerprint (AF) nanocoating market features various application methods, primarily resistance heating and electron beam heating. Resistance heating involves using electric currents to generate heat, which facilitates the coating process on surfaces, enhancing optical clarity and reducing glare. In contrast, electron beam heating employs high-energy electrons to rapidly deposit coatings, allowing precise control over thickness and uniformity. Both methods improve product durability and cleanliness, catering to the growing demand for advanced surface treatments in electronics and optics.</p></p>
