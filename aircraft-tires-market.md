<p><p>The aircraft tires market consists mainly of radial and bias tires, each serving distinct operational needs. Radial tires feature cord plies arranged perpendicular to the tread, offering improved fuel efficiency, reduced rolling resistance, and enhanced durability, making them popular for commercial aircraft. In contrast, bias tires have plies crisscrossing at angles, providing better load distribution and flexibility, often preferred for smaller aircraft and specific applications. Understanding their differences helps in selecting the appropriate tire type for optimal performance and safety.</p></p>
