<p><p>The diagnostic medical electrodes market includes three primary types: wet electrodes, dry electrodes, and needle electrodes. Wet electrodes utilize conductive gels or liquids to enhance signal transmission, making them suitable for applications like electrocardiograms (ECGs). Dry electrodes, which do not require any gels, offer convenience and comfort for long-term monitoring, reducing the risk of skin irritation. Needle electrodes feature invasive methods for more precise measurements in electromyography (EMG) and other applications, allowing for direct electrical signal access from tissues.</p></p>
